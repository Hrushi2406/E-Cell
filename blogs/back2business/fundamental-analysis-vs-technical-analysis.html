Fundamental Analysis Vs. Technical Analysis: The Clash of the Titans
by Nihar Joshi



The stock market is marred with mystery and uncertainty; it’s a strange world where there seem to be no method to the madness, no cap on the gains for those who play it right and mounting losses for those who remain on the other side of it. If one looks a little deeper though & it starts to make some sense. To make this transition, let’s look at a simple explanation of the top 2 jargons on the list for any stock market enthusiast; Technical analysis (TA) and Fundamental analysis (FA).


Fundamental analysis (FA) is a method to evaluate the intrinsic (internal) value of an asset and analyze all the factors that will influence the share price in the future. The term may sound exotic but simply put, what effect will factors like demand, taxes, business model, cash flows etc. have on the profits of a company and hence the share price. For instance, daily consumer products like bread, biscuits, dairy creamer and other bakery products are made by Britannia Industries. Will the demand for these products ever decrease? Absolutely not, in fact it keeps on increasing year by year which translates into more profits for the company. It also has a strong balance sheet with substantial cash inflows and low overall costs which is why it’s a market leader in its segment. Furthermore, if we take a look at the top management, they have solely focused on their core business which shows commitment and focus. So your money will be safe in this stock and it will deliver good returns in the long term. Voila! We have carried out a brief fundamental analysis of Britannia Industries.


Technical analysis (TA) is the study of historical data on prices and volumes to determine whether the current trend in the share prices will continue or reverse. Technical analysists believe that no external factors need to be evaluated because the share prices already take them into account and hence are sufficient to predict future prices. It consists of 2 factions; charts and technical indicators. The chart patterns are used to determine the trend lines and support and resistance levels for the share price. Support prices are those which provide a ‘support’ for the price and if this is broken the trend is downwards (Bearish). The resistance level provides ‘resistance’ when prices move up, if this level is broken then trend is upwards (bullish). Technical indicators like RSI, super-trend indicator, money flow indicators along with various candle chart patterns are heavily utilized by analysts and they provide a greater insight into the trend of the share prices but they are time consuming to learn and apply.


Both these analysis techniques require a lot of time to learn as well as to carry out. But this article is all about keeping it simple. So here’s a simple way to pick quality stocks, the top stocks in Nifty 50 index are regarded as best for a reason, so it is best to keep a watch on these and buy them whenever there is a significant decline. To estimate the best possible time and price, use a combination of TA & FA. They are tools to create wealth but don’t be obsessed with a single tool because FA give the right value while TA gives the right price. Remember that wealth in the stock market is built by investing in those companies which give a sustained & reliable return in the long term. If you are promised quick yet unreasonable returns like Ruchi Soya stock, it’s a trap. Mind you these top Nifty50 stocks in no way give a slow growth. With them you can double your money on an average every 3-4 years! If you don’t believe me, take a look at the share price of the top stocks Asian Paints, Bajaj Finance, HDFC for the last 10 years.
