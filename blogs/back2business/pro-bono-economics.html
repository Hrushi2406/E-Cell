PRO BONO ECONOMICS?
by Gargi Sadavarte



As rightly said by John .F . Kennedy- “The time to repair the roof is when the sun is shining.”


The Indian economy has had the distinction of being one of the fastest growing economies in the world over the last three decades. Yet, today there is a sense of economic malaise in the air. We as students also get frustrated when we see the margin created for employment sector. People are losing their jobs. Unemployment is on a high tide. Rural areas are in distress as farm incomes have stagnated. Corporate investment has declined. Banks burdened with unpleasant loans are finding it difficult to lend. Exports have declined. There are exhortation signs of the onset of a further slowdown.Travel restrictions and quarantines affecting hundreds of millions of people have left Indian factories short of labour and parts, just in time supply chains and triggering sales warnings across technology , automotive, consumer goods, pharmaceuticals and other industries.


The new strategies incorporated by the government are not grey. They are either black or white. The new reforms must contend with this charged atmosphere .In agriculture, the government decided to bypass “the states” rights and powers over agricultural marketing and open up trade outside previously existing, state – controlled wholesale yards. As with agricultural reorganisation, new codes governing the hiring and firing of workers have to be adopted. We have to revive the method of redundancy, which basically interprets to check post the methods and principles of the working organisation, so that accuracy in the outcomes is increased or maintained.


Following the policy choices of the government since the start of the pandemic, we have read a lot about how the government has not done enough on fiscal policies – that is – directly spending from its own account – and largely hoped that the RBI’s monetary policy via lower interest rates and more money for loans, would instead revive the economy ( which completely makes no sense, since we are already burdened with mountains of debts put on the banks by the so called “bad boy billionaires”.)


So what is it that we , as citizens want ?
Or is it just a phase which will pass in an eye’s blink!
The repair for economy is still a plausible phenomena which will very much happen , is the assurance given by the administration which we definitely want to believe and act upon being the youth generation of this country.
Ultimately, it is to be perceived by the reader whether the economy is supposed to be pro bono (for the public’s good) or not ?
